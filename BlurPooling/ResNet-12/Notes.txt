
Here is the breakdown of the 18-layer structure:

Initial Convolution: 1 (7x7 conv)
Residual Blocks: 16 (8 blocks, 2 convolutions per block)
Final FC Layer: 1 (Linear classifier)
Total: 1 + 16 + 1 = 18 layers with parameters. 


Building a Shift-Invariant ResNet-18 is the definitive way to apply Zhang’s research. In a standard ResNet, shift-invariance is broken in two places during 
downsampling:The Main Path: The strided convolution 3x3, stride=2 aliases the signal.
The Shortcut Path: The projection shortcut 1x1, stride=2 also aliases the signal.

We will fix both by inserting Bin-5 BlurPool layers.
## The Shift-Invariant ResNet Block:
This block replaces the standard Residual Block. Notice that strides=2 is removed from the convolutions and moved to the BlurPool layer.

2. Building ResNet-18 for EMNIST:
Standard ResNet-18 is designed for 224x224 images. For 28x28 EMNIST, we remove the initial max-pooling to preserve resolution for a few more layers.

## Why this is the "Ultimate" Test:
1. Skip Connection Sync: 
By blurring both the main path and the shortcut path, you ensure that the features being added together are spatially aligned. Without this, a 1-pixel shift could cause the shortcut and the main path to provide conflicting information.

2. Deep Feature Stability: 
ResNet-18 is deep enough that "aliasing noise" usually compounds. You should notice that your training loss becomes exceptionally smooth.

3. High Precision: 
Since you already hit 99.70% with simpler models, this ResNet-18 version is likely to be your most robust model yet, especially when dealing with "noisy" or "shaky" digit entries.
